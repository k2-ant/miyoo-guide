How to Use It

The basics take ten seconds: tap L/R to move between your game lists, A to play, B to back out. Everything else is a MENU shortcut — here's the whole map, with the moves worth knowing broken down below.

🧅 What "OnionOS" is — and why it matters

The menu you boot into isn't the plain Miyoo software — it's OnionOS, a beloved community-made overhaul that turns this pocket gadget into a tiny powerhouse.

🎛️ It runs everything

The menus, the box art, launching games, all the shortcuts — that's Onion. Under the hood it uses its own tuned build of RetroArch (the emulation engine) with a display driver made for this exact screen, so games look sharp and play smooth.

✨ It's the magic

Save-anywhere, instant sleep/resume, the GameSwitcher, Favorites, overlays, themes, even a playtime tracker — every nicety on here comes from Onion. It's why something this small feels this good.

🤚 It's already set up

Nothing to install or configure — it's all dialed in for you. One thing worth knowing: it all lives on the SD card. Treat that card kindly (don't wipe it), and if you ever move it to another Miyoo, your whole setup comes with it.

💾 Save states — your superpower

The single best thing about this little machine. A save state is a perfect snapshot of the exact instant you're in — and you can drop one anywhere, in any game. Loads of these old games had brutal checkpoints or no saving at all; save states fix that forever.

💾 Save a state

Hold MENU + tap R2. Snap — the exact moment is bookmarked, mid-jump or mid-cutscene, instantly.

📂 Load a state

Hold MENU + tap L2 to jump right back to that snapshot. Died? You're back on your feet in a second.

🔢 Multiple slots

Hold MENU + tap ←/→ to switch slots — a bunch of separate bookmarks per game, each with its own little thumbnail so you can tell them apart.
Made a mistake? Undo it open the RetroArch menu with MENU + SELECT

↩️ Undo Load State

Loaded the wrong slot and lost your spot? In the RetroArch menu (MENU+SELECT) pick Undo Load State — it drops you back exactly where you were right before you loaded. A genuine lifesaver.

↪️ Undo Save State

Accidentally saved over a good bookmark? Undo Save State restores what that slot held a moment ago — so one fat-fingered save won't cost you your progress.

😴 It auto-saves too

Tap POWER to sleep, or hold MENU to quit — Onion quietly saves a state either way, so you always pick up exactly where you stopped.

💡 Why it changes everything

Experiment freely — try a risky path and undo if it goes south. Beat any boss — save right before, retry instantly instead of replaying the whole level. Quit anytime — no hunting for save points; bookmark and walk away. Never lose progress on games that were never built to be forgiving. (One note: a save state is separate from a game's own in-game / battery save — both work, but states are the universal "anywhere" bookmark.)

⭐ Make it yours — Favorites

280 games is a lot to scroll. Favorites is your personal shortlist — the handful you're actually playing right now.

How to star one

Highlight any game and press SELECT — it pins to your Favorites list. Press SELECT again to un-star it.

Where they live

At the top of the menu, tap L/R to flip between Recents, Favorites & each console. Yours all sit under Favorites — no digging.

💡 The move

Star games as you discover them. Within a week you'll have your own "now playing" shelf and barely touch the full lists again.

⏯️ The GameSwitcher — bounce between games

The fastest way to juggle a few games at once — and the heart of OnionOS.

Open it

Tap MENU to bring up the GameSwitcher — a carousel of your recently played games, each showing a screenshot of right where you stopped.

Jump back in

Scroll with ←/→, press A, and you drop straight back into that game at the exact moment you left — it saves the spot for you automatically.

The shortcut

Already know which one you want? Double-tap MENU MENU to fly right back into the very last game without even looking.

⏩ Fast-forward — skip the slow parts

A lot of these old games love slow text crawls and grinding. Fast-forward blasts straight through it — once you know it, you'll use it constantly.

⏩ Speed it up

Hold MENU + tap R to toggle fast-forward — the game runs several times faster. Tap R again (still holding MENU) to switch it back to normal speed.

⏪ Rewind

Slipped off a ledge or walked into a hit? Hold MENU + tap L to rewind a few seconds and take it back (on cores that support it).

💡 When to use it

Long JRPG dialogue, level grinding, slow intros, backtracking across an overworld — hold fast-forward and reclaim hours. Ease off for anything that needs real timing.

🖼️ Overlays — make games look right

These old games were made for chunky TVs, not a crisp little LCD. Overlays are decorative frames — a CRT-screen look, or a console bezel — that wrap the game, fill the empty space on the sides, and bring back that authentic retro feel.

✅ Already switched on

Good news: tasteful CRT/bezel overlays are already enabled for each system, so your games look great out of the box. The steps below are only if you want to change one or turn it off.
Change or turn off an overlay set per system — it remembers your choice

1 · Open RetroArch

While in any game, press MENU+SELECT together to open the deeper RetroArch settings menu.

2 · On-Screen Overlay

Go to On-Screen Overlay. Flip Display Overlay on or off — or open Overlay Preset to pick a different frame.

3 · Make it stick

Back out one level to Overrides → Save Content Directory Overrides. That locks your choice in for that whole system, so every game on it matches.
