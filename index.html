ONIONOS — YOUR MENU SYSTEM

Working the Menus

The software is called OnionOS. The whole thing in four moves.

🎮 Find a game

Games are grouped by console. Tap L/R (shoulders) to flip between Recents, Favorites & each system. A plays, B backs out.

💾 Save anywhere★ THE BIG ONE

Hold MENU + the Save State button to bookmark the exact moment. Hold MENU + Load State to jump back. Save before bosses — or to quit mid-level.

⏯️ Tap MENU = GameSwitcher

One quick tap flips between your recent games. Hold it for the hotkeys →

⚡ Good to know

Hold MENU+ for Fast-Forward, Quick Menu, Exit & screenshots. Tap POWER to sleep/instant-resume. Multi-disc PlayStation: MENU → Disc Control.

⏳ HOW WE GOT HERE

A Quick History of Gaming

This handheld is a time machine. Here's the journey it covers, in five stops.

1977

Where it all began

The Atari 2600 brought arcade games home and lit the fuse. Pitfall, Adventure, Asteroids — the spark for everything that followed.

1985

The NES saves gaming

After a crash nearly killed the industry, Nintendo rebuilt it — Mario, Zelda & Metroid were all born here.

1991

The 16-bit war

SNES vs. Genesis. Mario vs. Sonic. The rivalry that pushed both to greatness — and gave us the classics.

1989→

Gaming goes portable

Game Boy → GBA put real games in your pocket. Tetris became the most-played game in history. (You're holding the heir.)

1994

The arcade's golden age

Where it was loudest and best — fighters, shmups & beat-'em-ups built to swallow your quarters.

1997

Games grow up & go 3D

The PlayStation brought cinematic storytelling and polygons. Gaming became a mainstream art form.
