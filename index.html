The Top 10

Out of hundreds of games, these are the ten you owe it to yourself to play. Every one is a certified, all-time, hall-of-fame classic. Start anywhere on this list and you literally cannot go wrong.

1

Chrono Trigger

SNES

If you play one game on this whole device, make it this. The greatest RPG ever made — and it's not close. Time travel, a dozen endings, not a single wasted minute. It turns people who "don't like RPGs" into believers.

2

Super Mario World

SNES

Thirty years on, nobody's topped it. Pure, perfect joy from the very first jump. Grab a cape, take flight, and you'll understand instantly why this is the platformer everyone measures against.

3

Zelda: A Link to the Past

SNES

The adventure that defined adventure. A massive world stuffed with secrets that still feels magical to explore. Boot it up "just to try it" and lose a whole weekend.

4

Super Metroid

SNES

It quietly invented an entire genre. Land alone on a dead alien world and feel the wonder and dread. One of the most atmospheric games ever made — trust it and let it pull you in.

5

Final Fantasy VII

PlayStation

The game that made the whole world take video games seriously. Three discs of spectacle and one story you will not forget. A cultural earthquake — and it fits in your pocket.

6

Metal Gear Solid

PlayStation

Stealth, intrigue, and twists people still talk about today. It plays like an action movie you control — and it was decades ahead of everything around it. Unforgettable.

7

Super Mario Bros. 3

NES

The blueprint for every platformer that came after — and still one of the very best. Raccoon suits, secret warp whistles, pure magic. This is where modern gaming was born.

8

EarthBound

SNES

A kid with a baseball bat against cosmic dread. Hilarious, strange, and secretly one of the most heartfelt games ever written. There is genuinely nothing else like it — give it an hour.

9

Tetris

Game Boy

The most-played game in human history, and maybe the most perfect ever designed. "Five minutes" becomes an hour every single time. Nobody is immune. Nobody.

10

Castlevania: Symphony of the Night

PlayStation

A sprawling gothic castle that's pure joy to explore and grow stronger in. So influential it named a genre. You'll never want it to end — and it goes on longer than you'd ever guess.

⏳ HOW WE GOT HERE

A Quick History of Gaming

This little handheld is a time machine — every era of gaming, in your hands. Here's the journey it covers.

1977

Where it all began

The Atari 2600 brought arcade games home and lit the fuse for everything that followed.

1985

The NES saves gaming

After a crash nearly killed the industry, Nintendo rebuilt it — Mario, Zelda & Metroid were all born here.

1991

The 16-bit war

SNES vs. Genesis. Mario vs. Sonic. The rivalry that pushed both to greatness.

1989→

Gaming goes portable

Game Boy → GBA put real games in your pocket. You're holding the heir to that whole line.

1997

Games grow up & go 3D

The PlayStation brought cinema and polygons. Gaming became a mainstream art form.
